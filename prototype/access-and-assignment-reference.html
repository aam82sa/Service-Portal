What happens if I change thispreview
Previewing a switch from Pull to Push — least loaded for IT. Nothing is saved until you press Apply.
34 officers across 6 teams start receiving requests automatically instead of choosing them.
34 currently-open requests keep their assignee. Nothing is reshuffled, reassigned or re-queued.
→ New requests only. The rule applies from the moment you apply it, to requests raised after that point.
7 officers are out of office and will be skipped until they return.
Who is affected
RequestersNo visible change. They still submit the same way.
OfficersWork is pushed instead of pulled. The “Claim” button disappears from the team queue.
Team leadsCan still reassign within their team, exactly as today.
Dept headsUnchanged. Reporting and approvals are not affected.
AdminsCan switch back at any time. The previous mode is kept in the audit log.
Reversible Switching back to Pull leaves every assignment already made in place — it only stops new ones being pushed.
× The global “Auto-assignment” feature flag is being removed It has been on since launch and nothing in the codebase ever read it, so turning it off changed nothing and turning it on promised something the product did not do. Distribution is decided per department and per team by the settings above. The flag will disappear from Feature flags on the next release; no migration is needed.
